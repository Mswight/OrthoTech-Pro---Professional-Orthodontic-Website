Our Commitment to Your Privacy

At OrthoTech Pro, we take your privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, protect, and share your personal information in compliance with HIPAA, GDPR, and other applicable data protection regulations.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

Personal Health Information (PHI)

  • Medical and dental history
  • Treatment plans and records
  • X-rays and diagnostic images
  • Insurance information
  • Prescription and medication records

Personal Information

  • Name, address, phone number, email
  • Date of birth and demographic information
  • Emergency contact information
  • Payment and billing information

Technical Information

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and version
  • Usage patterns and navigation history
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

  • Treatment: To provide orthodontic care and related services
  • Payment: To process billing and insurance claims
  • Healthcare Operations: To improve quality of care and operational efficiency
  • Communication: To send appointment reminders and treatment updates
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws and regulations
  • Research: For de-identified research purposes (with your consent)

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • Healthcare Providers: With referring doctors and specialists involved in your care
  • Insurance Companies: For claims processing and verification
  • Business Associates: With HIPAA-compliant vendors (e.g., cloud storage, billing services)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or court order
  • Emergency Situations: To protect your health or safety

We never sell your personal health information to third parties.

5. Your Rights

Under HIPAA and GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Request copies of your medical records
  • Right to Amend: Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Right to Restrict: Request limitations on certain uses of your information
  • Right to Accounting: Receive a list of disclosures we've made
  • Right to Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Deletion: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal requirements)
  • Right to Object: Object to processing for direct marketing

To exercise these rights, contact our Privacy Officer at privacy@orthotechpro.com

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience on our website. You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. Our cookies are used for:

  • Session management and authentication
  • Analytics and performance monitoring
  • User preferences and personalization
  • Security and fraud prevention

7. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, typically:

  • Medical Records: Minimum 7 years after last treatment (or longer as required by law)
  • Financial Records: 7 years for tax and audit purposes
  • Marketing Data: Until you opt out or 3 years of inactivity
  • Website Analytics: 26 months

8. International Data Transfers

If you are located outside the United States, please note that we may transfer your data to the U.S. where our servers are located. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for international transfers through:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Adequacy decisions by relevant authorities
  • Your explicit consent when required

9. Children's Privacy

We treat information about minors (under 18) with special care. For patients under 18, we require parental or guardian consent for treatment and data processing. Parents have the right to access, modify, or delete their child's information.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of material changes via email or website notice at least 30 days before they take effect.
